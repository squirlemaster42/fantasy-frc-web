# Scoring Explained

Understanding how teams (and your fantasy roster) earn points is key to Fantasy FRC success. This guide breaks down the scoring system in simple terms.

## üéØ Scoring Overview

Each FRC team you draft earns points based on their actual performance during championship events. Your fantasy score is simply the sum of all your drafted teams' points.

### Basic Formula
```
Your Score = Team 1 Points + Team 2 Points + ... + Team 8 Points
```

### Team Score Breakdown
Each team's total comes from four main components:
1. **Qualification Score** - Performance in early matches
2. **Playoff Score** - Elimination round performance  
3. **Alliance Score** - Championship alliance selection
4. **Einstein Score** - Einstein Championship performance (double points!)

## üìä The Four Score Components

### 1. Qualification Score ("Qual Score")
Points earned during qualification matches at championships.

**How it Works:**
- **Win** = 3 base points
- **Loss/Tie** = 0 points
- **Game Bonuses** = Additional points (varies by year's game)

**Example Match:**
- Team wins match + achieves 2 bonus objectives
- Points: 3 (win) + 1 (bonus 1) + 1 (bonus 2) = 5 points

**Why it Matters:**
- Most matches played = most scoring opportunities
- Consistent teams rack up points here
- Foundation of a team's total score

### 2. Playoff Score  
Points earned in elimination rounds (quarterfinals, semifinals, finals).

**Point Values by Round:**
- **Semifinals**: 15 points (upper bracket) or 9 points (lower bracket)
- **Finals**: 18 points
- **Quarterfinals**: 0 points (participation only)

**Bracket Explanation:**
- **Upper Bracket**: Teams that win their first quarterfinal series
- **Lower Bracket**: Teams that lose first but fight back through elimination

**Example:**
- Team wins semifinal in upper bracket = 15 points
- Same team loses finals = 0 additional points
- Total playoff score = 15 points

### 3. Alliance Score  
Points based on where teams get selected during championship alliance selection.

**How Alliance Selection Works:**
1. Top 8 teams become "alliance captains"
2. Each captain picks 3 other teams to join their alliance
3. Selection order matters for scoring

**Scoring Table (Base Values):**

**Example:**
- Team gets picked as 2nd pick by 3rd alliance
- Base value: 54 points
- Final score: 54 √ó 2 = 108 points

### 4. Einstein Score  
Special scoring for the Einstein Championship (the ultimate final).

**How it Works:**
- Same scoring as regular playoffs
- **ALL POINTS ARE DOUBLED**
- Only applies to Einstein Championship matches

**Example Einstein Scoring:**
- Regular semifinal win: 15 points
- Einstein semifinal win: 15 √ó 2 = 30 points

## ‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

**Do teams earn points if they lose?**
Yes! Teams earn points for wins and bonuses even if they lose the overall match. Alliance selection points also don't depend on match outcomes.

**What if a team gets disqualified?**
Disqualified teams earn 0 points for that specific match, but can still earn points in other matches.

**How are ties handled in standings?**
Most leagues use draft position as tiebreaker (earlier pick wins). Some commissioners may use other methods.

**Can scores change after being calculated?**
Yes, if match results are corrected or overturned, scores are automatically updated.

**Do teams from different events compete against each other?**
No, teams only compete within their championship division, but all teams' points count toward your fantasy total.

**What happens if my team doesn't make it to championships?**
Teams that don't qualify for championships earn 0 points in all categories.
